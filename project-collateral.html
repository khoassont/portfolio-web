Overview

In July 2025, the Faculty of Business and Economics finalised significant curriculum changes to their flagship degree, Bachelor of Commerce. I was tasked with coordinating an urgent project to update all collateral assets to reflect these course changes before the university's largest yearly student recruitment event in mid-August 2025.

Challenge
The problem As the curriculum changes were recent, many stakeholders within the faculty were still confused about their content. This led to conflicting editorial requests, confusing comments, and inaccurate feedback on mockups — all within a very compressed timeline.

The core challenge was not just updating assets, but doing so accurately under ambiguity. The risk of propagating incorrect course information through to prospective students at the recruitment event was significant.

Execution

To efficiently coordinate these efforts, I set out to become well-versed in all curriculum changes — directly consulting colleagues involved in the process to minimise confusion when liaising with stakeholders and the team.

From this, I synthesised a Single Source of Truth (SSoT) document to inform all collateral changes across the project. This document became the reference point for all editorial decisions and resolved conflicting stakeholder input.

I then organised cleanly annotated copies of each asset, incorporating stakeholder editorial requests, and sent these to both the internal design team and our CMS specialist. After many rounds of draft revision with the design team and faculty, I distributed the final mockups to relevant stakeholders before printing submissions.

Process solution Creating the SSoT document up front eliminated the back-and-forth caused by stakeholder confusion and gave the design team a single, reliable source to work from — significantly reducing revision cycles.
Results

All assets were updated accurately and delivered to the faculty's student recruitment team prior to the event, where they were showcased and distributed to prospective students.

Given the extremely compressed timeline, the outcome was deemed satisfactory by all stakeholders involved — with all 17 individual assets delivered on time and without content errors.
