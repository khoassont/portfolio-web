Overview

Nearing the end of 2025, stakeholders from the Faculty of Law expressed interest in expanding the faculty's presence in North America for their flagship post-graduate courses. With a limited end-of-year budget, I proposed a small-scale online marketing campaign as an experiment to test the viability of using student publications as a targeted channel overseas.

Screenshot — Banner example on student publication site
Rationale
01

Student publications primarily serve only one demographic: university students — the exact target audience for post-graduate course offerings.

02

Publications are usually university-specific, allowing for targeting of high-intent geographical areas and optimisation of creative messaging.

03

Online delivery through both websites and newsletters allows for flexible scheduling, budget-specific campaigns, and the ability to conduct AB testing — ideal for an experimental campaign with a smaller budget.

Research

I coordinated the campaign from ideation through to execution and metrics collection. Using admissions data collected by the university, I identified and synthesised a list of North American colleges with the highest admission rates — indicative of pre-existing student interest.

After inquiring about advertising rates from all potential publications, I cross-referenced pricing and potential reach to select the final candidates. From this, I presented a detailed proposal to faculty stakeholders, outlining preferred candidates and possible messaging and copy examples.

Execution

I negotiated rates with the publications' advertising teams to outline a marketing plan, splitting the budget 70/30 between web banners and newsletters. Leveraging a pre-existing cross-university partnership program, I chose a relevant landing page, synthesised custom UTMs, and wrote copy for the creatives.

Finally, I liaised with the internal design team to produce two simple creatives to be utilised for both the website and newsletters, before sending them off to the publications team.

Results

65k+

Total impressions over 2 weeks

36×

Higher CTR for newsletters vs web banners

8×

Lower CPC for newsletter placements

Key Insight Between 3 consecutive newsletters, clicks decreased significantly on the second and third deliveries — indicating audience saturation due to close scheduling proximity. This finding directly informs spacing strategy for future sends.

Overall, the experimental campaign achieved its goal of generating reach in a new market measured by impressions, while yielding valuable insights for potential future campaigns.
